## Mathematical & Computational Foundations

### [[P-vs-NP|P vs NP Problem]]
**Episodes:** 488, 475, 452
**Domains:** Mathematics, Computer Science, AI
**Question:** Can every problem whose solution can be quickly verified also be quickly solved?
**Implications:** Fundamental limits on computation, AI capabilities, cryptography

### [[Godels-Incompleteness-Theorems|Gödel's Incompleteness Theorems]]
**Episodes:** 488, 452
**Core Insight:** Any consistent formal system powerful enough for arithmetic contains true but unprovable statements
**Implications:** Limits of formal knowledge, truth vs proof, AI alignment challenges

### [[Cantors-Theorem|Cantor's Theorem & Infinite Hierarchies]]
**Episodes:** 488
**Core Insight:** Some infinities are larger than others; power set always larger than original set
**Implications:** Mathematical foundations, infinite complexity

### [[Halting-Problem|The Halting Problem]]
**Episodes:** 488, 475
**Core Insight:** Cannot determine if arbitrary program will halt or run forever
**Implications:** Undecidability, limits of AI predictability

### [[Computational-Complexity|Computational Complexity Theory]]
**Episodes:** 488, 475, 452, 447
**Framework:** Classification of problems by computational difficulty
**Categories:** P, NP, NP-Complete, NP-Hard, PSPACE, etc.

## Existential Risk & Safety

### [[Nuclear-Deterrence|Nuclear Deterrence & MAD]]
**Episodes:** 420, 389
**Doctrine:** Mutually Assured Destruction prevents nuclear war
**Reality:** 6-minute decision window, sole presidential authority
**Risk:** Speed overwhelming wisdom, accidental war, proliferation

### [[Great-Filter|The Great Filter & Fermi Paradox]]
**Episodes:** 455, 420, 452
**Question:** Why don't we see evidence of alien civilizations?
**Possibilities:**
- Filter behind us: Life/intelligence is rare
- Filter ahead: Civilizations destroy themselves
**Human Relevance:** Are we approaching our filter?

### [[AI-Alignment|AI Alignment Problem]]
**Episodes:** 452, 475
**Challenge:** Ensuring AI systems pursue human-intended goals
**Difficulties:**
- Specifying human values formally
- Scalability of alignment techniques
- Inner alignment vs outer alignment
- Deceptive alignment risks

### [[CBRN-Risks|CBRN Risks (Chemical, Biological, Radiological, Nuclear)]]
**Episodes:** 452, 420
**AI Context:** Models potentially enabling dangerous capabilities
**Testing:** ASL framework for evaluating risks
**Governance:** How to prevent catastrophic misuse

### [[Power-Concentration|Power Concentration]]
**Episodes:** 420, 452, 389, 442, 450
**Pattern:** Technology concentrating power in fewer hands
**Examples:** Nuclear authority, AI development, political power, economic wealth
**Challenge:** Safe management of concentrated power

## Epistemology & Truth

### [[Truth-vs-Proof|Truth vs Proof (Gödel)]]
**Episodes:** 488, 452
**Distinction:** Truth is larger than provability
**Implications:** Some things are true but unknowable within formal systems
**Application:** Limits of AI reasoning, mathematical foundations

### [[False-Memories|False Memories & Memory Unreliability]]
**Episodes:** 430, 464
**Finding:** Memory is reconstructive, not reproductive
**Implications:** Eyewitness testimony unreliability, narrative malleability
**Mechanism:** Reconsolidation changes memories upon recall

### [[Evidence-Problem|The Evidence Problem]]
**Episodes:** 446, 430, 488, 464
**Pattern:** Incomplete evidence across all domains
**Examples:** Archaeological preservation bias, memory gaps, mathematical incompleteness
**Epistemological Implication:** Humility about what we can know

### [[Media-Bias|Media Bias & Narrative Warfare]]
**Episodes:** 336, 424, 441, 442, 463, 464
**Observation:** All sides claim media bias against them
**Reality:** Truth is contested terrain in power struggles
**Challenge:** No neutral ground exists in ideologically charged topics

## Language & Cognition

### [[Language-Universals|Language Universals]]
**Episodes:** 426
**Finding:** 95% of languages fit verb-initial or verb-final with matching pre/postpositions
**Challenge:** To Chomsky's universal grammar
**Implication:** Usage-based rather than innate language structure

### [[Sapir-Whorf-Hypothesis|Sapir-Whorf Hypothesis (Linguistic Relativity)]]
**Episodes:** 426
**Question:** Does language shape thought?
**Evidence:** Mixed—some effects, not deterministic
**Example:** Piraha people lacking number words affects numerical reasoning

### [[Memory-Systems|Memory Systems & Processes]]
**Episodes:** 430
**Types:** Episodic, semantic, procedural, working
**Process:** Encoding → Consolidation → Retrieval → Reconsolidation
**Optimization:** Memory optimized for future prediction, not accurate past recording

**Episodes:** 488
**Application:** Cantor's theorem, Halting Problem, Gödel's theorems

## Cross-Cutting Concepts

### [[Limits-and-Boundaries|Limits and Boundaries]]
**Pattern:** Every knowledge system has constraints
**Examples:** Gödel (mathematics), AI alignment, archaeological evidence, nuclear decisions
**Implication:** Epistemic humility required

### [[Speed-vs-Wisdom|Speed vs Wisdom]]
**Pattern:** Decisions accelerating beyond wisdom capacity
**Examples:** 6-minute nuclear window, AI capability growth, geopolitical crises
**Challenge:** Human institutions evolved for slower timescales

### [[Emergence-Complexity|Emergence & Complexity]]
**Pattern:** Simple rules → complex behavior
**Examples:** Conway's Life, evolution, AI capabilities, civilization
**Question:** Is complexity fundamental or emergent?
